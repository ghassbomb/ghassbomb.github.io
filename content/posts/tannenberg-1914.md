# Briefing

The German Schlieffen Plan intended for the German Army in the east, the
8th Army under Prittwitz (initially), to hold off the Russians.
Meanwhile, the German army in the west would take France, and then pivot
east to support them against the Russians. The Germans expected an 1870
in the west, and a 1914 in the east; they got a 1914 in the west, and a
1870 in the east.

Two Russian armies, the 1st and 2nd, approach from the east and south
respectively. In reality (the scenario takes place right after war
breaks out), Prittwitz attempted to defeat the 1st army before the 2nd
army could support it, but failed miserably before he even got started.
When he requested permission to abandon East Prussia, he was sacked. But
he had good sense in attempting to defeat the Russians in detail. That
shall be our plan too.

We have to hold (or recapture, if lost) all our border positions until
the 14th turn.

# Order of Battle

We have on our side the 8th Army and a smattering of fortresses and
garrisons. The 8th army consists of: I Corps, XVII Corps, XX Corps,
Reserve Corps, 3rd Reserve Div, Thorn Reserve Div, and the 1st Cavalry
Div. Each Corps consists of: an artillery brigade, five infantry
brigades, and a cavalry regiment. The Russian's likely mimic this
structure.

1st Cavalry Div is stationed in the east in Gumbinnen, 20km to the east
of Stalluponen, directly resisting the Russian 1st Army. I Corps is
stationed in Insterburg, 40km west of them. This is the Insterburg
sector.

Some 100km south of that, I Reserve Corps is stationed in Angerburg.
This corps overlooks the Masurian Lakes. This is the Masurian sector.

Southwest, based in Allenstein, are the 8th Army HQ, and XX Corps. These
are another 100km from Angerburg. This is the Allenstein sector.

100km west of Allenstein, we have the XVII Corps based in Deutsch-Eylau.
This is the the DE sector.

Finally, we have the Thorn Reserve Corps in Thorn, and the 3rd Reserve
Div, holding off a Russian push west of the Vistula. This is the Vistula
sector.

# Battle of the DE-O-A Belt

## Turn 1

The Russians take the first turn. In the Insterburg sector, the Russian
1st Army attack our 1st Cavalry Div. They attack 60km south of
Stalluponen and drive a cavalry brigade into the forests. A little
north, more cavalry brigades push towards Stalluponen, but are kept in
check by our men. In the south, within the Allenstein sector, the 2nd
Army pushes north and arrives at the outskirts of Strassbourg and
Villenburg.

There is not much I can do in my turn. Most of my units have not
mobilized yet. I Corps has, though I don't think it advisable to move
them without more information on the enemy, and so I basically dig in
and skip the turn.

![The Russian lines of attack](https://i.imgur.com/LDBwvBZ.jpg)

## Turn 2

The Russians launch another attack in the east, in the Insterburg
sector. They don't make any gains along the road leading to the town,
but they scatter the 2nd Cav Brig of ours defending the southern flank
of the town. Some 100km south, in the Masurian sector, they capture the
town of Lyck and near Lotzen.

In the Allenstein and DE sectors, they arrive on the outskirts of
Tannenberg. They also take Willenburg. A cavalry brigade clashes with my
5th Hussars (XVII Corps Cav Reg) on the outskirts of DE, but are
checked, in our first engagement in this sector.

My first act is to pull the 1st Cavalry Div back to Gumbinnen, now that
Stalluponen is exposed, as it is an untenable position. The 1st Cavalry
Div is probably incapable of holding off the 1st Army alone, so I Corps
cannot be moved until their advance is checked. Checking the
reinforcements tab doesn't inspire confidence either. I can sympathize
with Prittwitz's position a little\...

Regardless, if a confrontation is inevitable, then I ought to do
everything I can avoid it. The main option, abandoning east Prussia, is
a bit risky, but there is nothing to be gained from meeting the
Russians, on their own terms. So, I transport I Corps to Osterode. 1st
Cavalry Div will just have to make do!

Meanwhile, I examine my reserve units and find that they are in fact
identical to my regular infantry. This means they can be used in
offensive operations, which is relieving. This also means that I have 5
corps worth of troops that can be deployed against the 2nd Army.
Suddenly, I'm not as scared anymore!

![The movement of I Corps](https://i.imgur.com/uRtvP6d.jpg)

## A Plan Emerges

The plan is thus: we give them another turn or two to burrow themselves
deeper into the hole, get more spread out and disorganized. Then, we
launch our pristine and well-arrayed troops in a complete encirclement
as envisioned below. A rough plan, but it might just work out.

3rd Reserve Div will move east of the Vistula and move towards the towns
of Lautenburg, Chorzele and Mlava. Capturing these three will basically
completely cut their supply lines completely and utterly. Thorn Reserve
Div will support them. I, XX, and XVII Corps will simply prevent the
Russians from moving to split East Prussia off, but when the time is
right will launch an all out attack. I Reserve Corps will defend the
Masurian sector.

The main issue is temporal: how long should I wait before attacking? The
troops along the Vistula are the only ones actually affecting any long
maneuvers, and I can estimate them taking 4-5 turns to achieve their
objectives (assuming no resistance). If I start moving them now, though,
they might chance upon some Russian troops and our whole plan will fall
into the gutter. I still don't have detailed intelligence on the enemy
troops either. Thus, I plan to wait one turn before finally attempt
crossing the Vistula.

After that, though, it's go time.

![My plan of attack](https://i.imgur.com/LaByUva.jpg)

## Enacting the Plan

So, I send various cavalry detachments to scout out enemy troops in the
DE-Allenstein sectors. XX Corps' 11th Dragoons move into Tannenberg;
outside the city, we see 3 infantry brigades of the Russian I Corps. I
move XVII Corps' 4th Lt. Horse into Gilgenburg as well. Beyond that,
well\... there's actually nowhere else to move them. The front is very
narrow, which will play into our hands when we trap them within it, but
it makes maneuvers otherwise impossible.

I also move the Graudenz reserve into Strassbourg, since only one of
those cities is under threat\...

The rest of my maneuvers concerned the I Reserve Corps; nothing big
there, just deploying them in forward positions.

## Turn 3

Turn 3 sees the Russians push deeper into the DE-Allenstein (henceforth
DE-A) sector. They've basically captured every town, save for DE,
Osterode, and Allenstein. With a corps in each of these, they form a
belt that will halt the Russian advance; henceforth the DE-O-A belt. I
spend most of the turn setting my troops around this belt.

The Russians attacks in the east are actually held off, and I manage to
counterattack even.

Meanwhile, in the Vistula sector, I order the two reserve corps to start
moving.

The Hindenburg-Ludendorff command also arrived this turn, and I sent
them off to Allenstein (where the old Prittwitz HQ was based).

## Turns 4 and 5

The Russians fall further into the trap. They attack across the front,
and they perform miserably in all battles both turns. I'm talking 0
casualties on our side,  20% losses on theirs. Where they win, they win
battles with such casualties that even Pyrrhus would blush! For a
second, I imagine that I'n transported to the Western front, and not in
the East.

Their only successes are, as expected, Insterburg sector. My troops
retreat to Intserburg in good order (I have them on Minimize Losses),
but the Russians\... they're in miserable condition! Even though they've
barely fought at all! But, by sheer number (and the fact they have
infantry and artillery, and we have only cavalry), they push on.

The Vistula Sector troops have captured Plock by turn 3, and are on the
outskirts of Lautenburg and Mlava by the 4th. I plan to station some
troops in the junction around Plock, to halt any Russian attacks from
Warsaw. I might still need a couple turns to secure the three objectives
for this group. This isn't much of an issue though, as over the next
turn, I see the Russians moving even their reserve divisions into the
DE-O-A belt. By the time my Reserve Corps are settled in, they will
either be completely routed or intensely (read: miserably!) attacking.

My original plan was to hunker down after cutting of their supply in the
DE-O-A belt, starve them out, but after these successes it seems smart
to launch an all-out attack. Our troops would be like a hot knife
through butter!

## Turns 6 and 7

![Progress around turn 6-7](https://i.imgur.com/htQTztd.jpg)

The Russians continue their attacks. In the Insterburg sector, they
almost encircle a Landwehr unit of ours, but we manage to hold on.

In the DE-O-A belt, they are practically grinding their heads against a
brick wall. In one pathetic attack on a fortress in the forests near
Willenburg, an entire Russian formation is annihilated (0 casualties for
the fortress)! These fortresses are dealing the Russians such heavy
blows and yet they keep attacking them.

Regardless, along the Vistula sector, by Turn 6, our cavalry have
captured all the objectives. We've only gotten infantry into Lautzenberg
and Mlava, but this is far faster than I predicted nonetheless. If they
counter attack, it would be a bit tough, but they will probably take a
turn or two to organize one (and by then it will be too late)!. Until
they can, they are completely cut off.

I decide to, finally, order an all out offensive in the DE-O-A belt to
pin their forces, buying the Reserve corps some time. The offensive goes
swimmingly well, but I think all of us could've predicted that! I feel
bad for my artillery, who have basically no use since the enemy is not
well-entrenched at all (he's barely organized!). By the end of turn 7,
the Russian troops in the belt are mostly encircled. They are not likely
have troops to deploy against my reserve corps. Thus, my victory is
essentially sealed.

![Progress around turn 6-7](https://i.imgur.com/ucTI2rt.jpg)

## Mopping up... sort of

![Progress around turn 8](https://i.imgur.com/NQocA0X.jpg)

The less said about the aftermath, the better. We completely annihilated
the 2nd Army, but did so in a very disorganized fashion. Only till turn
10 did I finish mopping up the enemy, and only by turn 12 were my troops
ready to pivot and attack the 1st Army. If I'd planned my exact attacks,
and the aftermath generally, more carefully, I would've been far
quicker.

Thus, the battle ended in a draw. In total, I had till turn 14 (7 weeks
in real-time) to defeat both 2nd and 1st Army, which is a bit harsh.
But, in hindsight, it would've been completely possible if I'd planned
ahead. For reference, Hindenburg defeated both armies in around 3 weeks
time.
