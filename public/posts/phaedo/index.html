Phaedo, by Plato

Ghassan Shahzad

January 6, 2025

Echecrates asks Phaedo to recount the death of Socrates to him. Phaedo tells us that, at his final moments, the following acquaintances were present:

Of native Athenians there were, besides Apollodorus, Critobulus and his father Crito, Hermogenes, Epigenes, Aeschines, Antisthenes; likewise Ctesippus of the deme of Paeania, Menexenus, and some others; Plato, if I am not mistaken, was ill.

Drawing attention to the fact that Plato was missing, given that Plato is the author of this dialogue, is likely the ancient equivalent of a ‘This is a work of fiction’.
