John Tiller's Seven Years War: 'Battle of Lobositz' AAR

2024-12-13
2 minute read
Wargaming

Background

On 29 August, 1756 the Prussian army under the command of Frederick the Great crossed the Saxon border. The Saxons gave up Dresden without a fight and, by the 9th of September, Frederick entered the city. He besieged the fleeing Saxon army around Pirna, but wished to starve them out rather than attack. He left a screening force to accomplish this task, and crossed into Bohemia on 30 September, moving through the Mittel-Gebirge.

As he made his way toward Lobositz, a scout informed Frederick that an Austrian army, under Count von Browne, was present at the exit, with its back to the River Elbe. I play Frederick, and attempt to defeat this army.

Deployment

Lobositz AAR

Plan

The Austrians are divided into a main force (the First and Second Line Inf. with substantial cavalry, artillery, and von Browne), a 2nd force (the Lacy Division and Advance Guard, along with the screen of Grenzers), and the 3rd force (cavalry brigades of O’Donnell and Lobkowitz) in the middle. Any attempt at enveloping the main or 2nd force will be difficult because of the natural terrain and because of the mobile 3rd force. Still, attacking frontally is not a viable option for the same reasons.

My plan is to concentrate our forces primarily on the 2nd force. Schwerin and Katzler’s cavalry will sweep through the Lobosich Berg, rout the Grenzers, and position themselves to envelop the 2nd force’s right flank. Meanwhile, von Preussen and Kleitz will position themselves in a J-shaped line, and fix the 2nd force’s line in turn, at which point Schwerin/Katzler will launch their attack.

The Austrians will launch a counterattack with their 3rd force, no doubt, and perhaps even with the main force. Katte’s Division and the bulk of our artillery (the rest will be at Lobosich Berg) will be positioned at Homolka Berg to deter and envelop any attack by the main force. The 3rd force will be the trickiest to handle — even if we succeed in routing the 2nd force, the cavalry can reverse all our gains in the disorder. The hope is that my own cavalry reserves (Kyau’s division) can react to any such maneuvers.

Events

An immediate headache was maneuvering the columns of troops. My plan assumed a lot more mobility than my troops possessed. It would have been better to plan deployments along roads. Advancing through the forests of Lobosich Berg was nigh impossible, and I had to maneuver around them.

Even attacking the Grenzers became a nightmare. First combat was in the woods between detachments of Schwerin’s cavalry and the Grenzers. My detachment was routed.
